Basic Information

Importance of the Liver

The liver is a vital organ responsible for filtering toxins, aiding digestion, and regulating metabolism. It produces bile which helps in breaking down fats and processes nutrients absorbed from food. The liver stores essential vitamins and minerals, regulates blood sugar levels, and detoxifies harmful substances. A healthy liver ensures balance, immunity, and overall well-being. Protecting liver health through early detection and proper lifestyle choices is essential for long-term survival.

Common Types of Liver Diseases

  • Hepatitis: Viral inflammation affecting liver function.
  • Cirrhosis: Severe scarring caused by long-term liver damage.
  • Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD): Excess fat buildup in liver cells.
  • Liver Fibrosis: Early stage tissue scarring.
  • Liver Cancer: Uncontrolled abnormal liver cell growth.

Suggestions to Prevent Liver Diseases

  1. Avoid excessive alcohol consumption.
  2. Maintain a balanced diet.
  3. Exercise regularly.
  4. Drink sufficient water daily.
  5. Get vaccinated against hepatitis.
  6. Schedule regular medical checkups.
